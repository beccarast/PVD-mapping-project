Drawn from a variety of experiences and grounded in the methodology of community-based participatory research, the Process Wheel evolved as the John Hope/Brown partnership grew and developed. Beginning with the collection of qualitative (such as staff and student preferences for certain crops over others and desire for more programming in a certain area) and quantitative (such as soil quality and type and shade patterns) data, each project goes through multiple stages involving the participation of Brown and John Hope students and staff. Projects range from infrastructure to events to education to community engagement. Some projects “cycle” through more than once as needs and desires change, while others are complete after they are implemented. The Process Wheel is imperfect, but has served as a useful model for phasing our projects.

The tire garden project is one example of how we have used the Process Wheel. The idea came from an initial site evaluation (ASSESS) and discussions between John Hope staff and students in the “Sustenance and Sustainability” course at Brown in the spring of 2012 about a designated outdoor gathering space with seating for students and teachers (BRAINSTORM). John Hope community members explained to Brown students that the design needed to address child safety, material cost, and site security (SOLICIT FEEDBACK). The students produced a final plan (REFINE). In May 2012, the students washed and installed ten used tires, which they salvaged at no cost, in a circular arrangement with a flowerbed in the center (IMPLEMENT) . When the summer began, John Hope students in the Early Learning Center were able to play and learn together in the tire garden. However, our project continued. As part of a larger qualitative survey conducted by a summer Brown student researcher about the use of outdoor spaces on site, John Hope staff communicated both that the black tires were overheating under the sun as well as a general desire for more color. In July, the student researcher painted them seven colors of the rainbow!
